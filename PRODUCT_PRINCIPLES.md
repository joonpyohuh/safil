# SAFIL — Product Principles

## Principle 1. Output First
Do not make the owner operate a complicated creation tool.
Default to a completed, usable output.

Bad:
- “Customize the tone, layout, keyword density, and campaign objective.”

Good:
- “딸기라떼 신메뉴가 나왔어요” 입력
- 게시 가능한 문구와 이미지가 바로 생성됨

## Principle 2. Every Output Includes a Reason
Each recommendation should explain:
- Why this wording was used
- Why this channel was selected
- Why this posting time is recommended
- Which café information influenced the result

The explanation must be optional and easy to understand.
The owner should not need expertise, but should be able to verify the logic.

## Principle 3. Never Promise What Cannot Be Guaranteed
Do not claim:
- Guaranteed top ranking
- Guaranteed sales increase
- Guaranteed follower growth
- Guaranteed review or search performance

Only state:
- What SAFIL created
- What the owner used
- What measurable change occurred
- What remains uncertain

## Principle 4. No Manipulative Marketing
Never provide:
- Fake reviews
- Keyword stuffing
- Spam comments
- Engagement manipulation
- Platform policy evasion
- Misleading claims

## Principle 5. Owner Has Final Control
AI should provide multiple strong options.
The owner selects the final result.

Do not auto-publish in the MVP.
Do not overwrite café identity with generic marketing language.

## Principle 6. Café Context Is a Product Asset
Store and reuse:
- Café concept
- Tone of voice
- Menu information
- Location
- Customer type
- Preferred wording
- Previously selected outputs

The product should become more personalized over time.

## Principle 7. Mobile-first, Low-friction
The primary user may be working inside the café.
Design for:
- One-handed mobile use
- Large tap targets
- Short forms
- Minimal typing
- Clear progress states
- Easy copy/share/download

## Principle 8. Trust Is the Main Product
Before shipping any feature, ask:

> 이 내용을 사장님에게 그대로 보여줄 수 있는가?

If the answer is no, do not ship it.
